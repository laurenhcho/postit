The Problem

CMU's campus is home to hundreds of student organizations, events, and opportunities, but students consistently miss out on things that they would have genuinely cared about. We found that the root cause is not a lack of information, but an excess of it delivered through channels that demand too much effort to parse through.

  • →Physical bulletin boards are visually overwhelming, with flyers layered on top of each other, no hierarchy, and no way to filter by what actually matters to each individual user
  • →Even when a student notices something interesting, there is no built-in mechanism to save it, follow up, or get reminded
  • →Students are getting more comfortable with algorithmically filtered, notification-driven feeds in every other area of their lives — a bulletin board simply can't keep up
  • →Clubs and organizations designed, printed, and posted flyers but see diminishing returns at their events

The broader gap

From a broader perspective, the bulletin board problem represents a larger gap between the physical infrastructure of campus communication that hasn't kept up with how students actually consume information. Students are getting more comfortable with algorithmically filtered, notification-driven feeds in every other area of their lives. Since a bulletin board can't keep up with the new technology, the mismatch is driving disengagement.

Our Solution

Our proposed solution is a digitized campus bulletin board that bridges this gap. At its core, the platform allows anyone to upload a flyer or create a post directly within the app. From there, data extraction pulls key information like the event name, date, time, hosting location, and relevant tags such as free food, arts, academic, or social.

  • →Browse clean, standardized cards in a filterable feed, or view the mix of traditionally designed flyers
  • →Browse by category, search by club name or hashtag, and save events you are interested in
  • →Connect your Google Calendar and set personalized reminder preferences — one week out, a few days before, or the morning of the event

The Goal

By understanding why students miss events they would have loved, and removing the friction that stands between curiosity and showing up, Post it! aims to do more than digitize a bulletin board. Our goal is to make campus feel smaller and more connected — one event at a time. Let's post it into a community worth being part of.
