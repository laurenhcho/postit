The problem

CMU's campus is full of events worth attending.
Students keep missing them.

The root cause isn't a lack of things happening — it's that the information is delivered through channels that demand too much effort to parse. Physical bulletin boards are visually overwhelming, with flyers layered on top of each other, no hierarchy, and no way to filter by what actually matters to each individual user. Even when a student notices something interesting, there is no built-in mechanism to save it, follow up, or get reminded.

The broader gap

Students are getting more comfortable with algorithmically filtered, notification-driven feeds in every other area of their lives. Since a bulletin board can't keep up with the new technology, the mismatch is driving disengagement.

Our solution

A data-driven digital bulletin board
that bridges the gap.

Anyone can photograph a physical flyer or create a post in the app, and our AI extracts the key details automatically. Students can browse a filterable feed, save events, and sync everything to their Google Calendar. The information that was always there finally reaches the people who wanted it.

Photograph any flyer

Snap a photo of any physical flyer. AI extracts the event name, date, time, location, and auto-tags it with categories like free food, arts, or academic.

⚡

Filterable feed

Browse clean standardized cards or original flyers. Filter by category, search by club name or hashtag, swipe to save.

📅

Google Calendar sync

Connect your Google Calendar and set personalized reminders — one week out, a few days before, or the morning of the event.

🗺️

Live campus map

See events plotted across CMU's campus in real time. Find what's happening near you right now or plan your week ahead.

🗂️

Event buckets

Create collaborative playlists of events and invite friends. When anyone in the bucket confirms, it shows up for everyone.

Follow organizations

Follow your favorite clubs and get notified every time they post a new event. Never miss an interest meeting again.

The goal

Building a campus that feels alive.

By understanding why students miss events they would have loved, and removing the friction that stands between curiosity and showing up, Post it! aims to do more than digitize a bulletin board. Our goal is to make campus feel smaller and more connected — one event at a time. Let's post it into a community worth being part of.
