Strategic analysis

The theory behind the platform

⏱️
Theory 01
Technology Acceptance Model
usefuleasysetup friction

TAM states that users' adoption of a new technology is based on their perception of its usefulness and ease of use. Our website clearly improves finding relevant CMU events and reducing missed opportunities — especially compared to scattered posters and social media posts. Although it may be unfamiliar at first, in the long run students may find it more convenient to consolidate all events on one site.

⚖️
Theory 02
Equity & Inclusion Framework
equal flooralgorithm bias

Those who benefit most are highly engaged, tech-savvy students and larger clubs. Those who may be disadvantaged are less digitally inclined students and smaller organizations. Moving events online wouldn't eliminate this issue completely, but it could help by giving more equal visibility to all events regardless of size.

🔗
Theory 03
Network Effects Theory
grows with usecold start risk

As more people upload their events, and more people use the website to find these events, the more useful the website becomes. If only a few clubs upload their events, it will become a cascade of people not using it — which is why our launch strategy partners with Student Affairs and high-visibility clubs to seed the platform.

📈
Strategic analysis
Competitive Advantage via IS
centralizedcost leader

Instagram stories vanish in 24 hours. Flyers depend on foot traffic. Post it! is always-on, searchable, and calendar-integrated. This places the platform at a narrow scope and as a cost leader — reducing friction for students and printing costs for clubs, strengthening the entire communication value chain on campus.

SWOT analysis
Strengths

Clear competitive advantage through accessibility and visibility. Filtering features and Google Calendar connection enhance the user experience and increase student engagement while reducing costs and environmental waste.

Weaknesses

Platform success relies heavily on student adoption. Low initial engagement could undermine effectiveness. Some organizations may find the upload process difficult and become discouraged.

Opportunities

Connections to more platforms such as Tartan Connect. Functionality can expand through more personalization and data analytics. Potential integration with official CMU calendar systems.

Threats

Competition from existing social media students currently use. Low initial engagement could prevent long-term sustainability. Technical difficulties with a centralized platform could undermine campus communication.
